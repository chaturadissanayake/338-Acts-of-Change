338 Acts of Change

This project is a powerful exploration into how visual communication can bring clarity to complex development data. It presents 338 initiatives that shaped Sri Lanka in 2024, using a spiral chart to represent a variety of projects aimed at strengthening communities, informing policy, and building resilience. Each dot in the chart represents an individual initiative that has contributed to the nation's sustainable development.

The project's design was driven by the necessity to bridge the gap between technical reporting and public understanding. In this increasingly data-driven world, transparency and clarity are vital. By transforming complex quantitative data into a cohesive visual representation, this work invites engagement and fosters an understanding of the progress being made in Sri Lanka’s development landscape.

The data presented comes from the UN INFO Platform – United Nations in Sri Lanka (2024), which categorizes 192 initiatives into key thematic areas: capacity development, service delivery, data analysis, partnerships, policy advice, and more. This breakdown offers a snapshot of the diverse efforts taking place across the country.

Ultimately, this visual project is more than just about counting the number of initiatives. It's about making development efforts visible and tangible for all stakeholders. The goal was to create a simple yet powerful visual language that prioritizes clarity and hierarchy, while maintaining a sense of fluidity and cohesion in its design.
