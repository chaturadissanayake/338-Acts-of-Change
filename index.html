Visualizing Sri Lanka's Development: 338 Acts of Change

A visual storytelling of Sri Lanka’s sustainable development efforts in 2024

Project Overview

This project explores the potential of visual communication to clarify complex development data. Featuring 338 initiatives that shaped Sri Lanka in 2024, this visualization portrays the diversity and impact of various efforts aimed at strengthening communities, informing policy, and building resilience. Through this engaging chart, we aim to demystify development work and highlight the incredible strides made toward sustainable growth.

Creating the Visual Narrative

The project’s design is born from a critical need to bridge the gap between technical data and its real-world implications. In today’s data-heavy environment, it’s crucial to communicate clearly and transparently. The 338 initiatives captured in this project are not just numbers—they represent stories of change, growth, and sustainability in Sri Lanka.

To create the visualization, I used a combination of tools including Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and Canva. These tools allowed me to bring together data, design, and interactivity. I also employed Flourish for creating dynamic and interactive charts, while the layout and styling of the web content were handled using HTML and CSS.

This project leverages data from the UN INFO Platform – United Nations in Sri Lanka (2024), which categorizes 192 initiatives into thematic areas like capacity development, service delivery, policy advice, and more. It’s not just about counting initiatives; it’s about understanding the underlying impact of these efforts on the country’s development.

The goal of this visualization is to make these efforts tangible and accessible, to allow the public, policymakers, and stakeholders to understand and engage with the progress being made. By using a cohesive and visually appealing format, this project seeks to engage a broad audience and empower them with the knowledge of ongoing development work.

The Power of Interaction

The interaction with the chart goes beyond just being a static visualization. I’ve integrated interactive elements, such as the GIF animation, that allows users to see the data evolve dynamically. This is especially important for conveying the relationships between different projects and how they contribute to the larger goals of sustainability.

Concluding Thoughts

The completion of this project is a testament to the power of design and visualization in conveying complex data in a digestible form. It's not just a chart; it’s a storytelling tool that brings Sri Lanka’s development progress into focus, allowing for better understanding and engagement.

Whether you’re a student, designer, or policymaker, this project serves as an example of how we can use design to better communicate development efforts. By making the data accessible, we empower individuals to engage with their community’s progress and contribute to shaping a sustainable future.
