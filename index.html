Chatura Dissanayake

Communications & Data Visualization Designer

338 Acts of Change

This project explores the potential of visual communication to represent complex development data in an accessible and impactful format. Centered around a spiral chart, the work captures 338 initiatives that shaped Sri Lanka in 2024—each dot representing a project aimed at strengthening communities, informing policy, or building resilience.

The design was inspired by a need to bridge the gap between technical reporting and public understanding. By translating quantitative data into a cohesive visual form, the project supports transparency and encourages engagement with development progress.

Drawing from the UN INFO Platform – United Nations in Sri Lanka (2024), the dataset categorizes initiatives into eight distinct thematic areas: capacity development, service delivery, data and analysis, partnerships and knowledge, policy advice, coordination, support functions, and other.

This work is not just about counting projects—it’s about making development efforts visible and tangible. The visual language prioritizes clarity, hierarchy, and simplicity while maintaining a sense of motion and cohesion.
